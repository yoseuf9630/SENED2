Frequently Asked Questions:

What kind of humanitarian aid does SENED provide?

SENED provides a variety of humanitarian aid to those in need, including:

1-Emergency Relief: Providing essential items like food, water, shelter, and medical supplies during crises. 2-Winter Aid: Distributing warm clothing, blankets, and heating supplies to help families stay warm during the winter months. 3-Support for Orphans and Refugees: Offering educational resources, healthcare, and psychosocial support to vulnerable children and families. 4-Development Projects: Implementing sustainable initiatives to improve living conditions and promote self-sufficiency in disadvantaged communities.

How can I donate to SENED?

You can donate to SENED through various channels:

1-Online: Visit our website and click the "Donate Now" button to make a secure online donation. 2-Bank Transfer: Transfer funds directly to our bank account using the details provided on our website. 3-In-Person: Visit our office during working hours to make a cash or check donation. 4-Other Methods: Explore other donation options like donating via phone or mail by contacting us through the information provided on our website.

Where does SENED operate?

SENED primarily operates in the Middle East, with a focus on providing aid to families in need in Tripoli, Palestine, Syria, and Yemen.

We also have a strong presence in Canada, where we connect Canadians with opportunities to support our mission.

Can I volunteer with SENED?

Absolutely! We welcome volunteers who are passionate about making a difference.

You can find information about volunteering opportunities and how to apply on our website's "Get Involved" or "Volunteer" page.
